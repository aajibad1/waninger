My passion for computer science began during my eight years of military service. I was primarily a small team leader in charge of a communications team with a very broad range of responsibilities. Together, we provided voice and data communications across Southeast Asia and here in the States. Working within United States Special Operations Command offered many privileges, one of which was access to the latest technologies. It was a blast but I continued to find gaps in what our restricted software list could provide. I continued to search for better and more efficient solutions that would be approved under our very strict security requirements. Ultimately, however, I realized that the most effective solutions would be the ones I developed from scratch.

I’ve spent the last several years building a foundation for Computer and Data Science at Portland State University and the University of Washington. Not only was I learning the basic traits of a computer scientist but continue to hone my interests in data science. The more math, statistics, and algorithms I have learned, the more fascinating the aspects of data science have become. My skills have been finely tuned for this career but where I really want to make a difference is mental health. Throughout eight years of military service I’ve gone through some very difficult times regarding mental health; both personally and with close friends and family. Mental health is a subdomain where I can use my combined passion and skillset to contribute to a cause in which I truly believe.
